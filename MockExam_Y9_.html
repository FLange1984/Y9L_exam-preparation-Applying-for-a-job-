English Y9 · Big Dreams – Small Steps

Mock Exam – Applying for a Job

Work in groups of 4. Use each tab to prepare, practise, and give peer feedback. One person can act as "teacher" for the follow-up questions.

Task Part 1 · Introducing yourself (1–2 min)

Prepare your self-introduction. Tick each point before you start speaking.

I mention my name, age / class, and where I'm from.
I describe 2–3 of my interests or hobbies.
I use character vocabulary to describe myself (e.g. hard-working, creative, patient).
I name the field of work I'm interested in and explain why.
I mention whether I have any work experience (e.g. babysitting, a school project, a part-time job).
I have a clear opening phrase and a closing phrase.
Phrase bank Useful phrases for Part 1
Opening
Hello, my name is … and I'm in Year 9. I'd like to tell you a little bit about myself. Let me start by introducing myself.
Describing yourself
People often describe me as … One of my strengths is … I consider myself to be … I'm also quite good at … I sometimes struggle with …, but I'm working on it.
Job interest & experience
I've always been interested in … What draws me to this field is … I would love to work as a … because … I have some experience with … through … I haven't had a formal job yet, but …
Closing
That's a brief overview of who I am. Thank you for listening. I'm happy to answer any questions.

Group activity: Each person has 90 seconds to give their introduction. The others listen and use the Peer Feedback tab. Afterwards: give 1 positive comment and 1 tip for improvement.

Task Part 2 · Pair dialogue (3–5 min)

Pick a job card below, then discuss the four points as if you are both waiting outside an interview room.

We discuss what we plan to say in the interview.
We talk about tasks and responsibilities of our jobs.
We mention skills and qualities needed.
We share expectations (hours, teamwork, challenges, motivation).
We ask each other questions and react to what is said – not just monologues!

Task Part 3 · Follow-up questions (1–2 min each)

One person in the group acts as the teacher and draws a question. The student answers spontaneously – aim for at least 2–3 sentences with a reason or example. The group then gives quick feedback.
